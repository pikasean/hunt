Gee, Square Pies
A British, Russian and American engineer were arguing about whose home country had the superior measurement system; and so, they decided to hold a pie contest in which each contestant will use their own country's measurement units to bake their own pie. However, in the end, the judges were not satisfied with any of the pies. When inquired, the judges replied, "Precision is key, but there seemed to be a lack of it. Perhaps the three of you were thinking of a different pie?" What pie could the judges be referring to?
The Yeast Dough
Ingredients
  • 3.1g instant dry yeast
  • 100ml warm milk (or warm water)
  • 2.7g white sugar
  • 2 eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 200ml milk (or water), at room temperature
  • 100g butter, melted
  • ~600g all-purpose/plain/white flour
Steps
In a cup, stir warm milk, sugar and yeast together. Let stand until foamy for about 10 minutes.
In a big bowl, crack eggs, add sugar, salt, milk, melted butter and stir together. Add sifted flour, yeast mixture and knead the dough until it’s smooth.
Cover the bowl with wet cloth, put in a warm place and leave to rise for 1 hour. After that time, knead the dough again. Repeat this step one more time.
The Filling
Ingredients
  • 310g fresh salmon, cut into small cubes
  • 100g basmati or jasmine rice, cooked
  • 270g mushrooms, sliced and fried
  • 1 big onion, sliced and fried
  • 4 eggs, cooked and chopped
  • 2+2 Tbsp finely chopped dill and parsley
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
The Pancake
Ingredients
  • 310ml full fat milk
  • 50ml warm water
  • 270g all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • a good pinch of salt
  • 2 tbsp of sugar
  • 2 tbsp of sunflower oil
  • 3.1 tsp baking soda
  • 2.7 tsp of lemon juice or vinegar
Steps
Break the eggs into a large bowl, add salt and sugar, whisk.
Add the oil and milk, whisk again.
Sift the flour into the bowl, whisk thoroughly.
Add warm water and whisk to incorporate any lumps.
Pour lemon juice or vinegar into a spoon with baking soda on it (to dissolve the soda), quickly add it into the batter and whisk a couple of times.
While the mixture is resting (it allows the ingredients to blend together better), heat the pan; grease the pan with some oil, if needed.
Evenly coat the pan with the batter. Cook about a minute, then flip the pancake and cook for 15-30 seconds more.
Keep prepared pancakes in a warm place.
The Glaze
Ingredients
  • 1 egg yolk beaten with 2 Tbsp milk, 1/2 tsp salt and 1/2 tsp sugar
Assembling
On a floured surface, roll out the dough to approximately 27cm*31cm rectangular and 6mm thick. You can roll the dough on a piece of baking parchment, thus it’ll be much easier to transfer the pie on a baking tray; moreover, you need to turn the pie upside down-the sealing should be on the bottom.
Coat the rolled dough with the pancakes.
Place the egg and herbs mixture lengthways down the centre of the dough.
Then arrange the mushrooms and onion mixture on top.
Next, arrange rice. And the last layer-salmon.
Cover the filling with pancakes, shape it to make a rectangular.
Then, fold the dough and seal the edges.
Transfer the pie upside down to a baking tray.
Decorate with pastry trimmings, and cut two slits in the top with a sharp knife.
Keep proofing for 27 minutes. Brush the pie with egg wash.
Bake in a preheated 200C/400F oven for 31 minutes or until golden.
Leave to cool slightly for 15 minutes before slicing.
Serve with a glass of milk or a cup of freshly brewed tea.
